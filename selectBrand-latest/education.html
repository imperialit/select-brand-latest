WHAT DOES THE SELECT BRANDS INSTITUTE OF WINES & SPIRITS DO?

  • Train and Educate individuals in the field of Wines and Spirits
  • Offer WSET Level 1, 2 & 3 certificate courses in Wines & Spirits
  • Certify professionals with an internationally recognized qualification
  • Actively develop and manage Wine & Spirits lists for our customers
  • Tailor-make goal-based incentive programs for our customers
Our Teaching Philosophy:

We know that “knowledge is power”, so we seek to develop the candidate’s knowledge of wines, through the use of class material and tutoring.

Knowledge paired with on-the-job experience will advance their career and stimulate their desire for more knowledge in the field. It is our mission to tap into the desire for information.

WSET LEVEL 1 AWARD: “CONFIDENCE FOR FRONT LINE STAFF”

WHO SHOULD ATTEND?

Wait staff who need a basic introduction to wines and wine enthusiasts

HOW WILL YOU BENEFIT?

The aim of the course is to give a basic introduction to wine with basic food & wine pairing.

(Successful students will receive a certificate and SBIWS Certificate of Completion)

COURSE OUTLINE?
  • What is wine
  • How wine is made
  • Get to know your major grapes varietals
  • Exploring popular style of wine
  • Serving and storing wine
  • Social responsibility
  • Basic wine & food pairing
  • The WSET Level 1 Systematic Approach to Tasting Wine

To gain the qualification, students need to pass a multiple-choice paper of 530 questions

WSET LEVEL 2 AWARD: “LOOKING BEHIND THE LABEL”

WHO SHOULD ATTEND?

Wait staff who need a basic introduction to wines and wine enthusiasts

HOW WILL YOU BENEFIT?

The aim of the course is to give a basic introduction to wine with basic food & wine pairing.

(Successful students will receive a certificate and SBIWS Certificate of Completion)

PROGRAMME OUTLINE?
  • How wine is made and other factors influencing the style of wine
  • Key white grape varieties
  • Key black grape varieties
  • Wine-producing regions of the world
  • Sparkling wines
  • Sweet wines and fortified wines
  • Spirits and liqueurs
  • Label terminology
  • Food and wine pairing
  • The WSET Level 2 Systematic Approach to Tasting Wine

To gain the qualification, students need to pass a multiple-choice paper of 50 questions
